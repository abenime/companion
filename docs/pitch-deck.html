The Problem
People do not log how they feel every day

Wellness apps usually depend on the hardest habit: remembering to check in.

When someone is tired, stressed, or overloaded, they are least likely to open an app and describe it clearly.

1

They forget

Manual mood tracking fades after the first few days, especially during busy weeks.

2

They notice too late

Stress often appears gradually through sleep, focus, movement, and screen habits.

3

The picture is incomplete

A phone app alone cannot see work patterns, rest patterns, and daily routine together.

The Whole System
How Companion works in simple words

It notices patterns, understands what changed, and suggests one helpful next step.

1

Notice

Your devices share approved signals like sleep, movement, screen time, work switching, and rest breaks.

2

Understand

Companion compares today with your normal routine, so advice is personal instead of generic.

3

Support

When patterns look risky, it gives a calm recommendation, chat explanation, or quick breathing reset.

The user does not need to diagnose themselves. The system turns everyday behavior into early, gentle support.

Privacy & Trust
Wellness support should not feel like surveillance

Companion uses signals, not personal content.

Users stay in control of what is collected, what is ignored, and what can be deleted.

No private content

No messages, screenshots, typed words, or personal documents.

User approval

Collection starts from clear permission and onboarding choices.

Sensitive app skip list

Users can ignore banking, password, work, or private apps.

Delete controls

Users can delete today's logs or purge all telemetry.

Trust is part of the core product06 / 10
