# Companion Pitch Deck Speaker Notes

## Slide 1: Title
Companion is a behavioral wellness app. Instead of asking people to fill out mood forms every day, it notices changes in daily patterns and gives support before stress builds up.

## Slide 2: The Problem
Most wellness apps rely on people remembering to check in. That fails when users are busy, tired, or stressed. The early signs are usually already visible in routine: sleep, movement, screen time, focus, and rest.

## Slide 3: The Whole System
The whole system is easy to explain: it notices, understands, and supports. It notices approved signals from daily life, compares today to the user's normal routine, and suggests one helpful next step.

## Slide 4: Why Different
Companion is not another mood tracker. It is passive, cross-device, and action-oriented. It does not just show charts; it explains what changed and gives a calm intervention the user can do immediately.

## Slide 5: Product Experience
The user sees a calm Wellness Orbit, simple energy and focus scores, a burnout forecast, a companion chat, and a guided breathing reset. The design avoids guilt, streak pressure, and alarm-heavy wellness UX.

## Slide 6: Privacy & Trust
The platform is designed around trust. It does not collect messages, screenshots, typed words, or personal documents. Users can approve signals, ignore sensitive apps, delete today's logs, or purge everything.

## Slide 7: What We Built
This is not just a concept deck. The project includes the mobile app, web dashboard, desktop behavior collector, AI wellness reasoning, notifications, privacy center, admin console, and subscription plan management.

## Slide 8: Demo
The demo should follow a simple path: consent and setup, dashboard view, insight explanation, and action. Show that a behavior change becomes a wellness insight, then becomes a useful intervention.

## Slide 9: Business Model
The model starts free, adds premium insights and interventions, then expands to organizations. Universities, workplaces, and wellness programs can use aggregate trends while respecting individual privacy.

## Slide 10: Vision
Close with the shift: wellness tools should not wait until someone asks for help. Companion turns behavioral patterns into early, privacy-preserving support.
